# Why so quiet? A health update.

I have not been too active on social media or github for a few months now.  Truth is since October 2018 I’ve been struggling with my health: specifically my lower spine.  Back in February 2008, I sustained a gym injury that herniated two discs.  Since then I went through years of physical therapy and medical procedures to minimize the pain and maximize movement.  I learned to lived with it even if it meant the occasional week of staying off my feet to soothe a flair up and relieve some pain.

In May 2017 I started experiencing some pain and reduction to mobility.  I connected with a surgeon and things were not severe enough for surgery then.  I began working with a sports medicine doctor and physical therapist.  I regularly saw these two for about 18 months.  I saw improvements in my health I never experienced before and felt great!  The October 2018 came and I felt things start to take a turn.  It was then I decided to incorporate a psychologist into the mix to help cope with some of the negative emotions as a consequence of the pain.

No amount of physical therapy, metal therapy, or spine manipulations offered relief.  Meditation and stretching helped but there will still stretches of sustained pain to the point that it became a constant in my life.  A new MRI in May 2019 introduced some new concerns.

![](images/slVOfjGzSJGNGaasHC9eog_thumb_3964.jpg)

The green? That is the damage that’s been there for a while: two discs without much water content resulting in darker imaging than other discs.  The red?  The white stuff hanging out around the two discs?  That’s a new problem.  So much as I spent two weeks worried my life was at risk.  Fortunately blood work confirmed it was not lethal but it still meant bad news: [spinal discitis](https://en.m.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discitis).  The blood work I had provided no evidence of viral or bacterial infection.  That is good because otherwise it meant three months of intravenous therapy drugs to try and resolve it.  The bad news is that it means the discs are in their final stages of life: they will slowly dissolve until the nearby vertebra naturally fuse.  This can take years and it will be significantly painful between now and then.

I now see a total nine different doctors on a regular basis.  I cannot express my disappoint with the American medical system or the medical accommodations process in Corporate America.  But lately I have taken more to meditation and practice grace.  It is not always enough but it has lifted my spirts and improved my outlook.  I am now admitted into a pain clinic, I continue to see my therapist, and recently had an epidural injection.  I still try to head out and see the occasional concert when my spine allows it.  I have backed off the donut crusade and focusing on eating better; it has contributed to dropping 30 pounds with plenty more weight to lose ahead.

Lately, though, life has started feeling normal again.  I have accepted and now learning to live with this new condition.  I have resumed my coding and studying sessions.  I restarted my mentorship and recruiting efforts at work.  And now I am back to writing regularly and taking to this new outlet on github to share updates with you.

Thanks for checking in.
